WHAT IS INSIDE

Five tools, one screen

01 · Competitor Intelligence

An hour of research in a two-minute read

The dashboard reads your competitors' recent posts and turns them into a short weekly brief: what worked for them, which opening lines stopped the scroll, and — most useful — what nobody in your niche is doing yet.

02 · Carousel Generator

Finished, branded carousels without a designer

You type one line — the idea or the hook. The dashboard plans the slides, generates the imagery in your visual style, and lays your text over it in your fonts and colors. You review everything, and you can change any word.

03 · Content Planner

See the month before you make anything

A monthly calendar of post ideas, color-coded by the kind of content you make. You can see at a glance whether the month is balanced — or whether you planned twelve product posts and nothing else.

04 · Hook Bank

Never start from a blank page

Every great first line you come across gets saved with its reusable pattern. Next time you write a post, you start from a bank of openers that already proved they work.

05 · Scheduler

Your publishing memory, where content is made

Write the caption, attach the media, pick the time. The queue shows everything that is going out and everything that already went — and posts to Instagram for you, on schedule.

WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE IN PRACTICE

A normal week is two focused hours

MONDAY

Read the weekly brief with your coffee. Two minutes tells you what moved in your niche.

MONDAY +20

Drop this week's ideas onto the Planner and see the shape of the week.

TUESDAY

Type the strongest hook into the Generator. Approve the plan, get finished branded slides.

WEDNESDAY

Write captions from saved hooks and queue the week's posts with dates and times.

REST OF WEEK

The queue posts for you. See a great hook in the wild? Save it in ten seconds.

The honest part: the dashboard does not make your content good — you do. It removes the friction: the research, the formatting, the design, the scheduling. Your judgment gets the two hours; the busywork gets none of them.
