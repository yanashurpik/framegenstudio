01 — Hero Model Recreation

Reference-match portrait — recreating the original campaign face reference.

Master Prompt — Front-Facing Hero Portrait

Extreme close-up beauty portrait of a young woman facing the camera dead-on, neutral
closed-mouth expression with a faint smize, wearing chunky tortoiseshell cat-eye sunglasses
with warm amber-tinted lenses and slightly upswept corners. Hair is dark brown, wet-look,
slicked straight back from a center part. Shot on an 85mm lens at f/5.6, eye-level, perfectly
symmetrical framing, cropped tight from hairline to upper chest. Lighting is a large soft frontal
softbox with gentle fill, even and shadowless, neutral 5500K daylight balance, soft catchlights
visible in the lens reflections. Skin shows real unretouched texture — visible pores, light natural
freckling across nose and cheekbones, faint sheen on the forehead, one small beauty mark near
the mouth. Chunky brushed-gold hoop earrings catch soft highlights. Black ribbed knit turtleneck
with visible rib texture at the collar. Background is a seamless light warm-grey studio backdrop,
completely flat and clean. Composition is centered and symmetrical with generous negative space
left and right. Styled like a contemporary luxury eyewear campaign — restrained, confident,
editorial-minimal — evoking quiet premium confidence.

02 — Product-Only Photography

Sunglasses as standalone hero — no model, three distinct treatments.

A — Clean White Background Hero Shot

Product photography of a single pair of chunky tortoiseshell cat-eye sunglasses, captured in a
three-quarter front angle, floating/standing upright on a seamless pure white background. Frame
is thick glossy acetate with an amber-brown-black mottled tortoiseshell pattern, cat-eye shape
with slightly upswept outer corners, warm amber-tinted lenses, visible gold-toned metal hinges at
the temples. Shot on a 100mm macro lens at f/8 for full sharpness front to back, straight-on
product angle, eye-level with the frame. Lighting is a large soft overhead softbox plus subtle fill
light from the front, fully diffused and even, producing a soft, short contact shadow directly
beneath the frame for grounding — no harsh reflections obscuring the acetate pattern, clean
specular highlights along the frame edges to show the glossy finish. Composition is dead-center
on the white field with generous negative space on all sides. Styled like a premium eyewear
e-commerce hero shot — crisp, true-to-color, no props, no hands. Mood: clean, covetable, precise.

Negative prompt: no model, no hands, no props, no background gradient or texture, no harsh glare on lenses, no visible dust/scratches, no warm or cool color cast, no soft focus

B — Suspended in Steel Wire Cables (Art Still-Life)

Product still-life photograph of a single pair of tortoiseshell cat-eye sunglasses with
amber-tinted lenses, suspended mid-air and entangled among multiple taut thin steel wire cables
crisscrossing the frame at irregular angles, as if caught in a web of tension wires. The frame tilts
diagonally, one lens facing toward camera, the cables threading both in front of and behind the
glasses to suggest depth. Shot on a 100mm macro lens at f/9 for full sharpness across both the
glasses and wires, slightly elevated three-quarter angle. Lighting is a soft overhead studio light
with gentle gradient falloff, brighter top-left fading to a deeper soft grey at the lower-right,
producing subtle directional highlights along the glossy acetate edges and crisp metallic glints
along each steel cable. Background is a seamless soft grey studio gradient with no visible horizon
line. Composition is dynamic and asymmetrical, the glasses positioned slightly left of center with
cables radiating outward through the full frame, generous negative space upper-right. Styled like
a high-concept luxury eyewear art-direction campaign — sculptural, tense, gallery-still-life energy.
Mood: suspended tension, precision, quiet drama.

Negative prompt: no model, no hands, no flat front-on catalog lighting, no cables fully obscuring the glasses, no harsh blown-out highlights, no color cast, no dust or fingerprints, no motion blur

C — Hand-Hold Detail (Red Nail Accent)

Product photograph of a single pair of tortoiseshell cat-eye sunglasses with amber-tinted lenses,
held delicately upside-down and at a diagonal tilt by an elegant feminine hand, fingertips with
glossy deep-red manicured nails gently pinching the frame's bridge and lower rim, the rest of the
hand relaxed and curved with fingers gracefully splayed. Shot on a 100mm macro lens at f/5.6,
eye-level, camera positioned to frame the diagonal composition running from upper-left to
lower-right. Lighting is soft and even from a large overhead softbox, producing a clean gradient
backdrop that's brighter white in the upper-left and softens into pale warm-grey toward the
lower-right, with subtle directional highlights along the glossy acetate edges and soft sheen on
the skin of the hand. Background is a seamless white-to-grey gradient studio backdrop.
Composition is dynamic and diagonal, glasses positioned upper-left with the hand extending from
lower-right, generous negative space in the upper-right quadrant. Styled like a premium eyewear
campaign hand-hold detail shot. Mood: refined, deliberate, quietly glamorous.

Negative prompt: no face/model visible, no busy or textured background, no harsh shadows, no dull or chipped nail polish, no awkward or stiff hand pose, no color cast, no fingerprints or dust on lenses

04 — Editorial Posing Set — Neutral Grey

5-shot pose sequence on warm neutral-grey seamless, same product.

Shot 1 — Low-Angle Close-Up

Close-up portrait shot from a low camera angle looking up at the model, framed from chin to
forehead. Pose: chin lifted and tilted slightly back, head turned a few degrees toward camera-left,
jawline and throat elongated, eyes lowered with a hooded, detached gaze down toward the lens.
Shot on a 50mm lens at f/2.8, camera positioned below eye-level tilted upward. Lighting is hard
directional light from the side, neutral warm-grey seamless background, strong rim light along the
jaw and cheekbone, high contrast with deep shadow under the chin. Tortoiseshell amber-tinted
frame catches a sharp highlight along the top edge. Composition is tight and asymmetrical, subject
filling most of the frame with minimal negative space above. Styled like a sculptural eyewear
campaign close-up. Mood: powerful, untouchable, commanding.

Shot 2 — Elevated Angle, Hand Gesture

Medium shot, camera positioned slightly above eye-level angled down at the model. Pose: torso
angled three-quarters to camera, head turned to look off to the side past the frame, one hand
raised near the jaw/chin with fingers elegantly curled and relaxed (not touching skin), shoulders
slightly dropped for a languid, confident posture. Shot on an 85mm lens at f/2.2, slight high angle
looking down. Lighting is soft frontal key light with gentle falloff, even neutral warm-grey seamless
background, subtle vignette at the edges. Gold hoop earring catches a glint of light. Composition
centered with the raised hand breaking the vertical line of the frame. Styled like a high-fashion
campaign still. Mood: aloof, deliberate, magnetic.

Shot 3 — Tight Close-Up, Glasses Pushed Up

Extreme close-up profile-leaning shot, sunglasses pushed up onto the forehead/hairline, exposing
the eyes looking upward. Pose: head tilted back and turned to a steep profile angle, chin raised
high, neck fully extended and exposed, eyes closed or rolled upward in a trance-like expression,
lips softly parted. Shot on a 100mm lens at f/2.0, camera at a slightly elevated angle looking down
the length of the face. Lighting is hard side light raking across the cheekbone and brow bone,
neutral warm-grey seamless background, deep shadow along the jaw, glossy highlight on the
tortoiseshell frame resting on the forehead. Composition is extreme and intimate, cropped tight
with the neck/throat as a strong diagonal line through frame. Styled like a dramatic editorial
eyewear insert. Mood: surrendered, sensual intensity.

Shot 4 — Three-Quarter Side Shot, Wearing

Three-quarter profile portrait, sunglasses worn normally. Pose: head turned to a clean
three-quarter angle away from camera, chin level and slightly lifted, shoulders squared but torso
angled, a calm and composed stillness — no exaggerated tilt. Shot on an 85mm lens at f/2.5,
eye-level. Lighting is even soft key light with a subtle rim light tracing the back of the head and
shoulder, neutral warm-grey seamless background, gold hoop earring fully lit and prominent.
Composition follows rule of thirds, subject positioned right of frame, generous negative space on
the left. Styled like a luxury eyewear campaign side profile. Mood: composed, self-possessed power.

Shot 5 — Full-Body Wide Shot

Full-body wide shot, model small within a large expanse of neutral-grey seamless background.
Pose: standing tall in a relaxed power stance, weight shifted onto one leg, opposite hip slightly
popped, arms resting naturally at the sides or one hand loosely grazing the hip, head turned
slightly to profile, chin level. Shot on a 35mm lens at f/4, eye-level, full figure centered low in
the frame with a tall expanse of negative space above. Lighting is a soft single-source key light
with a gentle vignette darkening toward the frame edges. Black fitted turtleneck reads as a clean
dark silhouette against the warm-grey field. Styled like a wide editorial campaign establishing
shot. Mood: solitary, monumental, cinematic scale.

Combined negative prompt (all 5 shots): no colored gel light, no red or saturated background, no flat even lighting, no smiling/open expression, no symmetrical relaxed pose, no visible logo distortion, no color cast inconsistency between shots

Pose-language throughline: chin always lifted or angled, never a straight-on neutral stance, plus an unbroken warm neutral-grey color cast across all five frames.

06 — Motion Intro — Storyboard v1

Static product reveal build — first draft, locked-off camera. Formula: SUBJECT / CAMERA / MOVEMENT / LIGHTING / MOOD / NEGATIVE — for Kling / Seedance image-to-video.

Shot 1 — Macro Texture Open (0–2.5s)

SubjectExtreme macro detail of the tortoiseshell acetate frame edge, amber lens catching light, no full glasses visible yet.
Camera100mm macro, static, locked-off.
MovementSlow rack focus pulling from soft blur into crisp focus on the acetate pattern; a single soft light flare drifts slowly across the lens surface.
LightingSoft top-down studio light, warm-grey seamless just visible out of focus behind.
MoodAnticipation, tactile, quiet build.
Negativeno camera shake, no fast motion, no full product reveal yet, no text overlay

Shot 2 — Suspended Rotation Reveal (2.5–5.5s)

SubjectFull pair of glasses suspended in space against the grey seamless, no hand, no model.
Camera85mm, static frame, subject centered.
MovementGlasses slowly rotate 180° on an invisible axis, light sweeping across the acetate and lens as it turns, like a product turntable but airborne and weightless.
LightingSoft overhead key with a subtle moving highlight that travels with the rotation, gentle gradient falloff on the grey backdrop.
MoodPremium, deliberate, hypnotic stillness in motion.
Negativeno spinning too fast, no motion blur smearing the frame, no background texture, no shadow flicker

Shot 3 — Hand Enters, Picks Up (5.5–8s)

SubjectAn elegant hand enters frame from the bottom edge, fingers gracefully closing around the temple arm of the suspended glasses.
Camera85mm, static, same framing as Shot 2 for continuity.
MovementHand rises into frame in one smooth continuous motion, fingers curl around the frame, slight settle/stop on contact.
LightingSame soft top-down key, consistent with prior shots, soft highlight on the hand as it enters light.
MoodHuman connection, intentional, grounding the product.
Negativeno jerky hand motion, no fingers obscuring the lens, no abrupt cuts within the shot

Shot 4 — Raised to Face / Put-On Motion (8–11s)

SubjectHand raises the glasses up and out of frame toward an implied face, or model lowers glasses onto her face from above.
Camera50mm, slight low angle, static.
MovementSmooth upward arc of the hand/glasses exiting frame-top, OR glasses lowering onto the model's face in one continuous downward motion, settling with a subtle micro-adjustment.
LightingConsistent soft warm-grey studio light, slight rim light catching the frame edge as it moves through the light.
MoodTransition from object to identity, anticipation resolving.
Negativeno face distortion, no double exposure ghosting, no lighting mismatch between shots

Shot 5 — Final Hero Hold (11–14s)

SubjectModel wearing the glasses, front-facing, neutral confident expression, same grey seamless and styling as the locked character shots.
Camera85mm, eye-level, centered, static.
MovementNear-static hold with only a barely perceptible breathing motion and soft blink — the landing frame for a logo/text card.
LightingSoft frontal key matching the rest of the set, even and shadowless.
MoodResolved confidence, the brand's signature held moment.
Negativeno movement large enough to disrupt text overlay placement, no expression change, no background shift
Continuity rule (all 5 shots) Same grey value and gradient direction in every shot, same light direction (soft top/front-left), same color temperature, no jump in lens reflection color between shots — this is what makes five separate generations read as one intro rather than five disconnected clips.

07 — Motion Intro — Dynamic Camera Revision

Shots 1–3 rebuilt with active orbit/push camera movement and stronger product motion.

Shot 1 — Macro Texture Open, Orbiting Push-In (0–2.5s)

SubjectExtreme macro detail of the tortoiseshell acetate frame edge and amber lens, no full glasses visible yet.
Camera100mm macro, mounted on a slow orbital dolly arc.
MovementCamera glides in a slow arcing push-in around the frame edge while simultaneously pulling closer, the acetate pattern sliding past in parallax as the angle shifts roughly 30°; focus sharpens progressively as the camera closes distance, light flare sweeping across the lens surface in sync with the orbit.
LightingSoft top-down studio key, warm-grey seamless drifting softly out of focus behind as the angle changes.
MoodAnticipation building into momentum, tactile, cinematic.
Negativeno shake or jitter, no whip-speed motion, no full product reveal yet, no text overlay

Shot 2 — Suspended Rotation Reveal, Camera Orbit (2.5–5.5s)

SubjectFull pair of glasses suspended in space against the grey seamless, no hand, no model.
Camera85mm, mounted on a continuous orbital track circling the product.
MovementCamera orbits smoothly around the suspended glasses in a 120–150° arc while the glasses simultaneously rotate on their own axis in the opposite direction, creating a layered double-rotation effect — light sweeps dynamically across the acetate and lens with each shift in angle, the product appearing to turn and float weightlessly through the full sequence.
LightingSoft overhead key with a moving highlight that travels continuously across the surface as both camera and product move, smooth gradient falloff tracking with the orbit.
MoodPremium, kinetic elegance, a true turntable-in-motion feel.
Negativeno rotation speed mismatch between camera and product, no motion blur smearing the acetate detail, no background texture, no shadow flicker or strobing

Shot 3 — Hand Enters and Lifts, Dynamic Tracking (5.5–8s)

SubjectAn elegant hand enters from the bottom edge, fingers closing around the temple arm and lifting the glasses up and slightly toward camera.
Camera85mm, slow push-in combined with a subtle downward tilt to track the lift.
MovementHand rises into frame in one continuous fluid motion, fingers curl and secure the frame, then lifts the glasses upward and angles them toward the lens in a confident sweeping arc as the camera pushes in to follow the motion, ending on a closer, more intimate framing than the shot opened on.
LightingSame soft top-down key, consistent with prior shots, highlight traveling along the hand and acetate as they move together through the light.
MoodHuman connection escalating into reveal, confident momentum.
Negativeno jerky or stop-start hand motion, no fingers obscuring the lens at the final frame, no camera-product movement desync, no abrupt cuts within the shot
Orbit continuity note Keep the orbit direction consistent (same clockwise or counter-clockwise rotation across both Shot 1 and Shot 2) so the eye doesn't get a visual reversal jolt at the cut. Shots 4 and 5 from Section 06 can remain static as a deliberate landing contrast, or be rebuilt in the same dynamic language for full consistency.

09 — Editorial Stills → Motion

Tilt-down drama and symmetrical hero stills converted to video shots.

Tilted Down, Dramatic Side Light

SubjectModel wearing tortoiseshell cat-eye sunglasses, head tilted down and turned, gaze cast away from camera in an aloof, detached expression.
Camera85mm, slightly elevated angle, static frame.
MovementHead tilts slowly downward and turns into the pose from a more neutral starting position, the hard side-light shadow sweeping across the face as the angle shifts, settling into the final tilted-down hold with a soft eyes-closing-then-lowering motion at the end.
LightingHard directional light from camera-right, the shadow line moving dynamically across the face as the head turns, deepening into the final high-contrast falloff on the left side.
MoodAloof, sculptural, quietly commanding.
Negativeno abrupt head snap, no flicker in the shadow edge, no eye contact with camera, no smiling, no lighting flatten-out mid-motion

Front-Facing Symmetrical Hero (Landing Beat)

SubjectModel wearing tortoiseshell cat-eye sunglasses, front-facing, direct gaze, neutral composed expression.
Camera85mm, eye-level, centered, static frame.
MovementNear-static hold with only a barely perceptible breathing motion and a single soft blink partway through — the landing beat where the face settles into stillness after the previous shot's movement.
LightingSoft even frontal key held constant throughout, no shift or flicker.
MoodResolved, composed, direct confidence — a held final beat.
Negativeno head movement, no expression change beyond the blink, no lighting drift, no background shift
Sequencing note These two work as a closing pair — tilted/shadowed motion resolving into the still symmetrical hold gives a natural settle beat. Flip the order (hero hold first, tilt-down second) to use them as an opening pair instead, shifting the emotional arc from calm establishing shot into rising drama.
